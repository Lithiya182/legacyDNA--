SPONSOR EVALUATION AND STRATEGIC PARTNERSHIP REPORT
Subject: Evaluation of Corporate Partnerships for TechFest 2022
Report Date: December 5, 2022
1. Overview
The sponsorship drive for 2022 aimed to pivot from general monetary donations to "Engagement-Based Partnerships." While the total revenue targets were met, several operational friction points were identified in the way corporate partners were managed.
2. Detailed Sponsor Performance
Apex Dynamics (Platinum Sponsor - $12,000)
Reliability: High. Funds were transferred on schedule.
Communication: Excellent. Provided their own marketing assets and branding guidelines early.
Issues Encountered: Apex requested a private recruitment room that was not in the original contract. This caused a last-minute scramble to vacate a faculty lab.
Recommendation: For 2023, include "Private Recruitment Suites" as a specific line item in the Platinum Tier package.
Silicon Stream Solutions (Gold Sponsor - $8,000)
Reliability: Moderate. Payment was delayed by three weeks, causing cash-flow issues for the marketing team.
Communication: Poor. Frequently changed the personnel assigned to our committee, leading to conflicting instructions regarding their stall placement.
Issues Encountered: Their branding banners arrived in the wrong dimensions for the auditorium stands.
Recommendation: Require a 50% upfront deposit for all Gold and Platinum sponsors before any marketing materials are printed.
EcoCharge Energy (Silver Sponsor - $4,000)
Reliability: High.
Communication: Proactive.
Issues Encountered: Not enough foot traffic was directed toward their booth because it was placed behind the main stairwell.
Recommendation: Map out sponsor stalls using a heat-map of expected foot traffic and ensure Silver sponsors are in high-visibility corridors.
Local Food & Beverage Partners (In-Kind - $4,000 value)
Reliability: Low. One vendor pulled out 24 hours before the event.
Communication: Chaotic.
Issues Encountered: Over-reliance on "verbal agreements" led to a shortage of water bottles on Day 1.
3. Recurring Problems in Sponsorship Outreach
The "Last-Minute" Pattern: Many local businesses expressed interest but stated the outreach happened too late for their annual budget cycles.
The "Value Gap" Pattern: Sponsors felt their logos were too small on digital flyers compared to the amount of money contributed.
The "Deliverable" Pattern: We failed to provide sponsors with a post-event "Impact Report" showing the reach of their brand, which has made it harder to sign them for next year.
4. Recommendations for Future Outreach
Actionable Step: Begin the sponsorship drive in June (6 months prior) to align with corporate fiscal planning.
Actionable Step: Create a "Sponsor Branding Kit" that specifies exact pixel dimensions and banner sizes to prevent printing errors.
Actionable Step: Appoint a dedicated "Sponsor Success Manager" whose only job is to ensure every contracted deliverable (booth space, logo size, speaking slot) is met.
Actionable Step: Within 14 days of the event, send a customized PDF report to every sponsor with photos of their booth and attendance data.
