ANNUAL POST-EVENT EVALUATION REPORT: TECHNOVA 2022
Event Title: TechNova 2022: The Future of Autonomous Systems
Date: November 18–20, 2022
Location: University Central Campus & Engineering Block
1. Executive Summary
TechNova 2022 marked the university’s first full-scale return to an in-person technology festival following two years of hybrid and remote iterations. The event focused on robotics, autonomous vehicles, and machine learning. Despite a successful turnout, the event highlighted significant gaps in logistical planning and volunteer management that required immediate attention for future cycles.
2. Attendance and Engagement
The festival saw a total of 2,850 unique attendees.
Student Participants: 1,900 from the home university; 750 from 18 visiting institutions.
Industry Delegates: 120 recruiters and engineers.
Keynote Attendance: The opening ceremony featured a standing-room-only crowd of 1,200 in the Main Auditorium.
Workshop Participation: The "Embedded Systems 101" workshop was oversubscribed by 40%, indicating a high demand for practical, hands-on hardware training.
3. Budgetary Performance
The total approved budget was $35,000.
Revenue: $38,200 (Sponsorships: $28,000; Registration Fees: $7,500; University Grant: $2,700).
Expenditure:
Technical Equipment & Kits: $12,000 (34% of budget)
Hospitality & Catering: $9,500 (27% of budget)
Marketing & Branding: $6,000 (17% of budget)
Prize Money: $5,000 (14% of budget)
Miscellaneous/Contingency: $2,700 (8% of budget)
The event concluded with a surplus of $3,200, which has been transferred to the Student Innovation Fund.
4. Event Highlights
The Robo-Sprint 2022: A high-speed obstacle course for autonomous rovers. This was the most-watched event and secured local media coverage.
Industry Networking Night: A successful "speed-dating" style session between senior engineering students and 15 partner companies.
Sustainable Tech Showcase: 25 student projects focused on renewable energy were exhibited in the Central Quadrangle.
5. Operational Challenges and Problems
Logistics Failures: The transport of heavy robotics equipment from the storage facility to the competition arena was delayed by four hours on Day 1 because the rented transport vehicles were not large enough for the crates.
Volunteer No-Shows: We experienced a 30% attrition rate in the volunteer workforce on Day 2. Most cited lack of clear instructions and exhaustion as the reasons for not returning.
Technical Bottleneck: The online registration portal crashed three times during the 48-hour period leading up to the event due to high traffic, leading to approximately 150 frustrated participants who had to be registered manually.
6. Lessons Learned (Patterns)
Communication Pattern: There was a consistent pattern of information silos between the Finance Team and the Logistics Team, leading to duplicate orders for supplies.
Attendance Pattern: High-energy competitive events (like robotics) saw 90% more engagement than theoretical panel discussions.
Procurement Pattern: Ordering technical components from overseas vendors less than six weeks before the event led to three key shipments being stuck in customs, requiring expensive local emergency purchases.
7. Recommendations for Future Organizers
Actionable Step: Establish a "Logistics Audit" 48 hours before the event to verify vehicle sizes and load capacities.
Actionable Step: Implement a "Volunteer Training & Certification" program where students must attend a mandatory briefing session to receive their event credentials.
Actionable Step: Move the registration system to a scalable cloud-based server (AWS or Azure) at least one month prior to the launch.
Actionable Step: Assign a dedicated "Liaison Officer" to sit between Finance and Logistics to approve all expenditures over $200.
